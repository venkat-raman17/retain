Below is how I’d design the content system.

1. The big idea: every day is an initiation

Most apps say:

Day 1, Day 2, Day 3…

Retain should say:

Day 1 — The Vow
Day 2 — The First Gate
Day 3 — The Fire Rises
Day 4 — The Eye Must Be Trained
Day 5 — The Body Must Be Included
Day 6 — The Lie of “Just Once”
Day 7 — The First Rite

Each day should contain a complete “formation unit.”

Daily unit structure

Each day should include:

Title
Opening line
Teaching
The day’s command
Practice
Journal question
Forge challenge
Evening account
One-line seal

Example:

Day 11 — The Fire As Messenger

Opening

The urge is not always asking for release. Sometimes it is announcing unused life.

Teaching

When the fire rises, ask what part of you has gone unfed. Is it the body needing movement? The soul needing silence? The mind needing work? The heart needing brotherhood? Compulsion offers one answer to every form of discomfort. A man learns to hear more deeply.

Command

Do not obey the first interpretation of the urge.

Practice

When desire rises today, ask: “What is this energy really asking me to build, face, clean, move, or repair?”

Journal

What need hides beneath my strongest urges?

Forge challenge

Turn one urge into 20 minutes of physical movement or meaningful work.

Evening account

Did I treat the fire as command, enemy, or messenger?

Seal

The fire speaks. The man interprets.

That is how every day becomes meaningful.

2. The 90-day progression should have “seasons”

Instead of 90 random passages, make the journey feel like a masculine formation arc.

90-day Retain path
Days 1–7: The Vow

Theme: beginning, identity, privacy, no shame, the first pause.

Goal:

“I am not trying to become pure. I am trying to become governed.”

Milestone: Day 7 — The First Gate

Days 8–14: Guard the Gates

Theme: attention, eyes, imagination, phone, fantasy, first spark.

This is critical. Confucian thought treats ritual as a way of cultivating virtue partly by restricting desires and shaping conduct; this maps beautifully to Retain’s “boundaries are not cages, they are training forms.”

Milestone: Day 14 — The Gatekeeper

Days 15–21: The Body Must Be Included

Theme: training, sleep, food, posture, breath, environment.

The app should teach:

A disordered body will ask for false comfort.

Milestone: Day 21 — The Forge Opens

Days 22–30: The Discipline of Assent

Theme: Stoic pause, impression vs. consent, command over reaction.

Stoicism is perfect here because it gives Retain one of its strongest ideas: the first impulse is not the final authority. The Stoics distinguished impressions from assent; an impression can appear without the person being forced to agree with it.

Milestone: Day 30 — The Oath Renewed

Days 31–45: The Inner Order

Theme: Plato, reason/spirit/appetite, the inner kingdom.

Plato’s Republic famously presents the soul as having reason, spirit, and appetite; Retain can translate this into a non-academic inner-order model: appetite has power, spirit has courage, reason must rule.

Milestone: Day 45 — The Inner Throne

Days 46–60: Transmutation

Theme: desire into work, service, creation, prayer, training, craft.

This is where the app becomes uplifting:

The question is not only “What did you avoid?”
The question is “What did you build with the fire?”

Milestone: Day 60 — The Quiet Strength

Days 61–75: Brotherhood and Service

Theme: isolation, friendship, responsibility, mentorship, repair.

Men do not only need restraint. They need belonging, usefulness, and honorable bonds. This is also where Retain protects itself from becoming self-obsessed.

Milestone: Day 75 — The Brother’s Hand

Days 76–90: The Integrated Man

Theme: mature command, humility, future, leadership, long path.

Existentialist philosophy is useful here: it emphasizes authenticity, responsibility, and the task of becoming through chosen action rather than passive conformity.

Milestone: Day 90 — The Crown of Command

3. Make milestones feel like rites, not badges

Badges are cheap. Rites are remembered.

A milestone should include:

A name
A short ceremonial passage
A self-audit
A vow renewal
A Forge challenge
A lock-screen-worthy line
Example milestone
Day 30 — The Oath Renewed

Thirty days is not the summit.
It is the proof that the man can live by a chosen word.
The fire has risen. The body has argued. The old patterns have spoken.
Yet here you are.

Audit

What has become easier?
What still hunts me?
What boundary saved me?
What boundary must be strengthened?
What did I build with the fire?

Vow renewal

I do not worship the streak.
I honor the practice.
I return to command.

Forge challenge

Complete one difficult act today before pleasure: train, clean, build, study, serve, or repair.

Seal

The oath is not a sentence. It is a spine.

4. The archetypes should be strong, but mature

Each archetype should not be a costume. It should be a mode of formation.

Every archetype needs:

Light
Shadow
Discipline
Temptation
Practice
Lapse medicine
Daily command
The core Retain archetypes

I’d use 12 archetypes, not just 6.

1. The Monk

Essence: silence, restraint, sacred attention.
Shadow: withdrawal, superiority, disgust toward the body.
Discipline: solitude without fantasy.
Temptation: hiding from life and calling it purity.
Practice: 10 minutes of silence before screens.
Command: Guard the inner room.

Retain line:

The Monk does not hate the world. He refuses to be invaded by it.

2. The Warrior

Essence: courage, endurance, decisive action.
Shadow: aggression, ego, domination.
Discipline: train when the body wants comfort.
Temptation: mistaking anger for strength.
Practice: one hard physical act daily.
Command: Move before you negotiate.

Retain line:

The Warrior does not spend fire in fantasy. He carries it into the contest.

3. The Craftsman

Essence: patience, skill, repetition, excellence.
Shadow: perfectionism, hiding in preparation.
Discipline: turn urge into craft.
Temptation: consuming instead of creating.
Practice: 30 minutes of deep work.
Command: Make something real.

Retain line:

The Craftsman knows that energy becomes noble when it enters the hands.

4. The King

Essence: order, responsibility, protection, judgment.
Shadow: control, pride, entitlement.
Discipline: govern the small kingdom: room, schedule, speech, body.
Temptation: wanting authority without self-rule.
Practice: restore one area of order daily.
Command: Rule yourself before you rule anything else.

Retain line:

The King’s first kingdom is his own attention.

5. The Lover

This one is important. Without it, Retain can become dry and anti-life.

Essence: beauty, reverence, tenderness, presence.
Shadow: lust, fantasy, possession, sentimental weakness.
Discipline: see beauty without consuming it.
Temptation: confusing desire with devotion.
Practice: appreciate without grasping.
Command: Revere, do not consume.

Retain line:

The Lover is not ruled by appetite. He is trained to behold.

6. The Pilgrim

Essence: humility, journey, return, learning.
Shadow: aimlessness, self-pity, endless restarting.
Discipline: keep walking after imperfection.
Temptation: dramatizing the fall.
Practice: one clean action after every mistake.
Command: Return without theater.

Retain line:

The Pilgrim is not the man who never falls. He is the man who keeps the road.

7. The Sage

Essence: discernment, study, truth, self-knowledge.
Shadow: overthinking, detachment, intellectual pride.
Discipline: learn the pattern beneath the urge.
Temptation: reading instead of practicing.
Practice: one insight, one action.
Command: Know the pattern. Break the spell.

Retain line:

The Sage does not ask only what happened. He asks what kept happening.

8. The Brother

Essence: loyalty, honesty, accountability, warmth.
Shadow: dependence, approval-seeking, tribal grievance.
Discipline: reach out before isolation rots.
Temptation: suffering silently to protect pride.
Practice: send one honest message.
Command: Do not disappear.

Retain line:

A brother’s hand is sometimes the difference between a lapse and a return.

9. The Guardian

Essence: protection, boundaries, vigilance.
Shadow: suspicion, rigidity, fear.
Discipline: guard the gates without hating the world.
Temptation: blaming temptation instead of building boundaries.
Practice: remove one trigger from the environment.
Command: Close the gate early.

Retain line:

The Guardian wins before the battle becomes dramatic.

10. The Builder

Essence: purpose, structure, future, work.
Shadow: workaholism, identity through output only.
Discipline: give energy to a worthy project.
Temptation: escaping into planning.
Practice: advance one real project today.
Command: Build what makes you harder to enslave.

Retain line:

The Builder gives desire a destination.

11. The Healer

Essence: repair, mercy, integration, restoration.
Shadow: softness without standards, endless self-analysis.
Discipline: repair without excusing.
Temptation: using pain as identity.
Practice: clean one wound: apology, order, sleep, confession to journal.
Command: Repair what you can.

Retain line:

The Healer does not erase the wound. He stops worshiping it.

12. The Sovereign

This is the integrated archetype.

Essence: command, balance, mature freedom.
Shadow: spiritual ego, isolation at the top.
Discipline: hold all parts in order.
Temptation: believing one victory makes him finished.
Practice: daily account.
Command: Remain governable by the good.

Retain line:

The Sovereign is not desireless. He is undivided.

5. Philosophy-backed ideas to include

These should become Retain’s “deep teachings.”

A. Plato: the inner kingdom

Use Plato’s reason/spirit/appetite model.

Retain translation:

Appetite has force.
Spirit has courage.
Reason must give direction.
A man becomes disordered when appetite takes the throne.

Feature:

Inner Kingdom Check-In

What does appetite want?
What does spirit feel?
What does reason command?
What action restores order?

This gives men a powerful mental model without shame.

B. Stoicism: the pause before assent

Stoicism gives Retain its most useful daily mechanic.

Retain translation:

The urge appears.
Consent is yours.

Feature:

Assent Drill

When an urge appears:

This is an impression.
It is not yet a command.
I do not have to agree.
I choose the next act.

This directly supports the app’s founding line:

Manhood begins in the pause between urge and action.

C. Aristotle: virtue through repetition

Aristotle’s ethics are built around becoming a certain kind of person through habituated action; virtue is not merely a feeling, but a trained disposition. The Stanford Encyclopedia’s treatment of Aristotle’s ethics emphasizes character, practical wisdom, and the role of habituation in moral development.

Retain translation:

You do not become disciplined by admiring discipline.
You become disciplined by repeating disciplined acts.

Feature:

The Repetition Ledger

Not just streak:

urges paused
Forge Acts completed
boundaries kept
returns after lapse
promises kept

Daily line:

Character is carved by repetitions too small for the ego to celebrate.

D. Confucius: ritual shapes the man

Confucian thought is useful because it treats ritual not as empty performance, but as a formative structure for desire, conduct, respect, and virtue. The Stanford entry notes that ritual can shape values by restricting desires and allowing reflection.

Retain translation:

A ritual is a chosen form that trains wild energy.

Feature:

Morning Rite / Evening Rite

Morning:

stand
breathe
read vow
choose command
name likely temptation

Evening:

account
repair
gratitude
vow renewed

Daily line:

Without ritual, desire invents the day.

E. Nietzsche: self-overcoming, but purified

Nietzsche is dangerous if used immaturely because it can feed ego, contempt, or domination. But one idea is useful: the man must overcome his lower forms rather than merely obey inherited comfort. The Stanford entry notes Nietzsche’s concern that asceticism can become psychologically destructive and self-defeating, which actually supports Retain’s “transmutation, not self-hatred” guardrail.

Retain translation:

Do not destroy the fire. Give it a higher form.

Feature:

Self-Overcoming Challenge

What part of me wants the old comfort?
What stronger form is asking to be born?
What act today proves the higher form?

Guardrail:

Retain does not teach contempt for weakness. It teaches transformation of weakness into practice.

F. Existentialism: responsibility and authorship

Existentialism helps men who feel drift, meaninglessness, or passivity. It emphasizes authenticity, responsibility, and the courage to choose a life rather than dissolve into the crowd.

Retain translation:

No one can choose your command for you.

Feature:

Author the Day

Morning prompt:

If today became evidence of the man I am becoming, what would it show?

Evening prompt:

Did I live authored or automated?

Daily line:

A man is not only what he wants. He is what he repeatedly chooses.

G. Brotherhood: the antidote to silent collapse

Retain should not become a solitary cave. Men need bonds, service, and honest witness. Modern loneliness research makes this essential, and the Surgeon General’s advisory work has emphasized the importance of meaningful social connection.

Feature:

Brotherhood Prompt

Offline app, no social network needed:

Call a brother
Send a truthful message
Ask for help
Offer help
Repair one bond
Leave isolation for a public place

Daily line:

Isolation makes the urge louder. Brotherhood gives the man back his name.

10. Add “crisis-aware” but non-clinical content

Because men are in real pain, Retain should have sober passages that say:

This app is a practice companion. It is not emergency help.

But within daily content, it can still be strengthening.

Examples:

When he feels worthless

You are not the worst thing you did in a weak hour.
You are the one who can stand up now.

When he is isolated

Do not let pride turn solitude into a locked room.
Send the message. Step outside. Let another human voice reach you.

When he lapses

Do not make a religion out of the fall.
Study it. Repair one thing. Return.

When he feels rage

Anger is often a guard dog standing over grief.
Do not let it bite the innocent. Train it toward protection.

When he is tempted at night

Darkness makes bad counsel sound intimate.
Stand up. Turn on the light. Leave the room. Breathe where the body cannot bargain.

12. The deepest idea to include

This should become one of Retain’s central teachings:

Men are not saved by information alone.
Men are formed by repeated acts under meaningful symbols.

That is the difference between Retain and a habit tracker.

A habit tracker counts.
Retain initiates.

A habit tracker asks, “Did you fail?”
Retain asks, “What did the fire reveal, and what will you build with it?”

A habit tracker resets the man to zero.
Retain says:

Stand up. The Path continues.
