# Content strategy

> "Men are not saved by information alone. Men are formed by repeated acts under meaningful symbols."
> — The core distinction between Retain and a habit tracker.

## The founding idea: every day is an initiation

Retain's daily content is not a stream of tips. It is a formation arc. Each day presents a complete "formation unit" — a title with ceremonial weight, an opening line that frames the teaching, the teaching itself, a specific command for the day, a practice, a Forge challenge, a journal prompt, an evening account, and a seal (a one-line summation the man can carry with him).

The model draws on a simple observation: initiatory traditions across cultures move men through structured experiences that combine intellectual formation (teaching), behavioral engagement (practice), and reflection (journal). The daily unit does all three in a form that works offline and in under five minutes of reading.

## The 90-day seasonal arc

Rather than 90 random passages, the path is structured as a masculine formation arc in eight seasons. Each season has a theme and a culminating milestone rite.

| Days | Season | Theme |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1–7 | The Vow | Beginning, identity, the first pause, no shame |
| 8–14 | Guard the Gates | Attention, the phone, fantasy, the first spark |
| 15–21 | The Body Must Be Included | Training, sleep, food, the body as partner |
| 22–30 | The Discipline of Assent | Stoic pause, impression vs. consent, inner kingdom |
| 31–45 | The Inner Order | Plato, reason/spirit/appetite, inner governance |
| 46–60 | Transmutation | Desire into work, service, creation, prayer |
| 61–75 | Brotherhood and Service | Isolation, friendship, repair, responsible bonds |
| 76–90 | The Integrated Man | Mature command, humility, authorship, long path |

The seasons ensure that the practice deepens over time — it does not repeat the same "be strong" message for 90 days. It moves a man through attention, body, philosophy, transmutation, and finally integration.

## Milestone rites: passages, not badges

Milestones at days 7, 14, 21, 30, 45, 60, 75, and 90 are not achievements to collect. They are ceremonial passages with real content: a ceremonial passage (spoken as if addressed to the man), a self-audit (specific questions demanding honest answers), a vow renewal, a Forge challenge, and a seal.

The difference between a badge and a rite is weight. Badges are cheap — they can be skipped, mocked, or forgotten. A rite requires the man to stop, account, and recommit. Retain builds rites that take five minutes and leave something behind.

## The 12 archetypes

Each day is assigned an archetype — a mode of formation, not a costume. The archetypes are:

**Monk** · **Warrior** · **Craftsman** · **King** · **Lover** · **Pilgrim** · **Sage** · **Brother** · **Guardian** · **Builder** · **Healer** · **Sovereign**

Each archetype has an essence, a light and a shadow, a discipline, a temptation, a daily practice, lapse medicine, a daily command, and a "Retain line" — the sentence that captures its relationship to the practice.

The Lover archetype is worth noting: without it, Retain risks becoming dry and anti-life. The Lover is present to beauty without possessing it. Reverence, not consumption. He is essential to the product's philosophical balance.

The Sovereign is the integrated archetype — the destination, not the starting point. A man who holds all parts in order, not desireless but undivided.

## Philosophical influences and how they are used

Each philosophical lineage is used for a specific insight, framed as inspiration rather than authority:

- **Stoicism** — The pause before assent. Impressions vs. commands. The most direct philosophical backing for the core mechanic.
- **Plato** — The inner kingdom: reason, spirit, appetite. A map for the Days 22–30 season.
- **Aristotle** — Virtue as trained disposition. Character carved by repetition. The "Repetition Ledger" idea.
- **Confucius** — Ritual shapes desire. The morning and evening rites. Form as formation.
- **Nietzsche (carefully)** — Self-overcoming, not self-contempt. His own concern that asceticism becomes psychologically destructive directly supports Retain's "transmutation, not self-hatred" guardrail.
- **Existentialism** — Authorship and responsibility. The "Author the Day" prompt.
- **Brahmacharya / Buddhist sense restraint / Daoist alchemy / Tibetan inner fire / Western alchemy** — All framed as historical/philosophical inspiration. Each tradition is paired with a guardrail.

## Content safety guardrails

See [CONTENT_SAFETY.md](CONTENT_SAFETY.md) for the full rulebook. In summary:

- No medical claims (no testosterone, fertility, depression, anxiety, performance guarantees).
- No shame mechanics (no failure identity, no purity/impurity language).
- No misogyny or grievance framing.
- No explicit sexual technique (karmamudra, karezza, partnered tantra, etc.).
- No religious authority claims — only philosophical/historical inspiration.
- Every philosophical tradition paired with a guardrail.
- Lapse content is always recovery and learning, never punishment.

The content-safety scanner in `src/testing/content-safety.ts` blocks degrading language and the content test runs it against all bundled content on every build.

## Voice and register

- Aspirational but not grandiose.
- Masculine but not hostile.
- Mystical but not medical.
- Direct but not cruel.
- Non-shaming throughout.
- Reads as if spoken to a younger brother you believe in.

See [CONTENT_SAFETY.md](CONTENT_SAFETY.md) for allowed and disallowed copy examples.

## V1 content scope

- 10 principles
- 7 Codex days (the foundational weekly arc)
- 30 full daily path entries (days 1–30, Seasons 1–4)
- 3 Studies (researched, guardrailed)
- 3 Rituals (morning, evening, temptation-hour)
- 12 archetype profiles
- 12 journal prompts
- 8 milestone rites (days 7–90, with full schema)
- Non-medical disclaimer + crisis resources

Days 31–90 are the planned expansion for v1.1. The schema and seasonal structure are in place; the content follows.
